Who I Am

I'm a Data Science and Economics student at the University of California, San Diego with a deep interest in data analytics, machine learning, and applied economic analysis. I enjoy working with real-world datasets to uncover patterns, build predictive models, and communicate insights through data.

My experience includes analyzing housing affordability trends using Zillow and U.S. Census data, where I built time-series forecasting models (ARIMA/SARIMA) to study changes in housing costs relative to income. I've also worked with large platform datasets to explore factors influencing customer ratings and operational performance.

I primarily work with Python, SQL, and statistical methods using tools such as pandas, NumPy, and statsmodels for data cleaning, analysis, and modeling.

I'm drawn to the intersection of data science and economics — areas like market analysis, economic forecasting, and using data to inform real-world decision-making. I'm also interested in machine learning applications, NLP, and building tools that make data more accessible and actionable.
